1. Introduction

1.1 About Unjob.ai

Unjob.ai is an AI-powered, content-first freelance platform that connects creative freelancers (creators) with companies and brands seeking high-quality creative work.

Unlike traditional job boards, Unjob.ai removes the need for résumés or lengthy proposals. Instead, it uses Smart AI Matching to pair freelancers with projects based on their skills, style, and tools showcased in their portfolio.

Key characteristics of Unjob.ai include:

  • No résumés or cover letters – creators simply upload their work (e.g., reels, designs, animations, case studies, user-generated content).
  • AI-based matching – connects companies with creators based on creative style and past work, not just keywords.
  • Two-way hiring – companies can pitch projects directly to creators whose work they admire.
  • Secure payment process – brands fund the project upfront, and freelancers are paid after work is submitted and approved, avoiding delays or confusion.
  • Portfolio-first approach – creators can build an easily viewable portfolio for companies to browse.

In essence, Unjob.ai is designed to simplify and modernize the way creative professionals and companies find, collaborate, and complete projects.

1.2 Purpose of the Manual

This manual is a comprehensive step-by-step guide for using Unjob.ai, covering all major features, tools, and functions available to both freelancers and hiring companies.

The purpose of this manual is to:

  • Guide users through each section of the platform — from account creation to posting gigs or projects.
  • Explain the functionality of every button, option, and menu item on the website.
  • Provide clear instructions for completing common tasks, such as uploading a portfolio item, creating a gig, applying to a gig, reviewing applications, and managing payments.
  • Offer troubleshooting tips for common issues users may encounter.
  • Help maximize platform efficiency — ensuring freelancers showcase their work effectively and companies find the best talent quickly.

This manual includes visual references (screenshots, icons, labels) for better clarity, ensuring that even new users can quickly adapt to the platform without prior experience.

1.3 Who Should Use This Manual

This manual is intended for two main audiences:

Freelancers / Creators

  • Individuals offering creative services such as video production, social media content creation, graphic design, animation, and related work.
  • Freelancers seeking a portfolio-based, AI-matched way to find projects without the traditional job application process.

Companies / Hiring Managers / Brands

  • Organizations, marketing teams, startups, and brands seeking skilled freelancers for creative projects.
  • Companies looking to post gigs, browse portfolios, and quickly identify suitable candidates.
  • Employers who value an efficient, transparent payment process and direct collaboration with creators.

Unjob.ai offers a set of core features that cater to both freelancers (creators) and hiring companies (brands) to ensure smooth collaboration, efficient matching, and secure project completion.

2. General Platform Mechanics & Essential Information

2.1 How Unjob.ai Works – Full Workflow Overview

Unjob.ai follows a streamlined workflow that emphasizes visual portfolios and AI-driven matching over traditional application processes.

For Freelancers (Creators):

  1. Register & Profile Setup – Create an account, add skills, upload profile photos, and write a bio.
  2. Create Posts – Upload your creative work (videos, designs, case studies) as posts on your profile.
  3. Build Portfolio – Select your best posts to feature in your portfolio for maximum visibility.
  4. Browse & Apply to Gigs – Search for relevant projects and submit applications with portfolio samples.
  5. Receive Direct Pitches – Companies may approach you directly based on your portfolio.
  6. Project Execution – Complete work, communicate with clients, and submit deliverables.
  7. Get Paid – Receive payment in your Unjob.ai wallet after project approval, then withdraw to your bank account.

For Companies (Brands):

  1. Register & Company Setup – Create a company account with business details and verification.
  2. Fund Wallet – Add money to your Unjob.ai wallet for project payments.
  3. Create Gigs – Post project requirements, budget, timeline, and assets.
  4. Browse Creators – Search portfolios and directly message suitable freelancers.
  5. Review Applications – Use green/red icons to accept or reject applicants.
  6. Hire & Collaborate – Hire creators, communicate project details, and track progress.
  7. Approve & Pay – Review deliverables and release payment upon satisfaction.

Key Differentiator: The AI matching system analyzes your content style, tools used, and project history to suggest the most relevant opportunities or candidates.

2.2 Unjob.ai vs. Other Platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, etc.)

Understanding how Unjob.ai differs from traditional freelance platforms helps users maximize their experience.

Traditional Platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, 99designs):

  • Proposal-Heavy: Require detailed cover letters and bidding processes.
  • Keyword Matching: Matching based primarily on search terms and ratings.
  • Complex Fee Structures: Multiple fee tiers, service charges, and payment delays.
  • Limited Visual Focus: Text-heavy profiles with minimal emphasis on creative portfolios.

Unjob.ai Advantages:

  • Portfolio-First: Visual content takes priority over written proposals.
  • AI-Powered Matching: Smart recommendations based on creative style and compatibility.
  • Direct Pitching: Companies can approach creators directly, reducing competition.
  • Transparent Pricing: Clear, upfront payment structure with escrow protection.
  • Creative Focus: Designed specifically for visual and creative industries.
  • Faster Hiring: Streamlined process from discovery to project completion.

3. Getting Started

This section covers the initial steps to begin using Unjob.ai, including creating an account, logging in or out, and navigating the dashboard for both freelancers and companies.

3.1 Account Registration – Freelancers

To work on Unjob.ai as a freelancer (creator), you must first create a freelancer account.

Steps to register:

  1. Open the Unjob.ai homepage in your preferred web browser.
  2. Click Sign Up in the top-right corner of the page.
  3. On the registration form, select Freelancer as your account type.
  4. Fill in the required fields:
    • First Name and Last Name
    • Email Address (use a valid email for verification and communication)
    • Password (must meet the platform's security requirements)
    • Confirm Password
  5. Check the box to agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.
  6. Click Register.
  7. Verify your email by clicking the confirmation link sent to your inbox.

After registration, complete your freelancer profile:

  • Upload a Profile Photo and Cover Photo.
  • Write a Bio describing your skills, creative style, and professional background.
  • Select your skills from the available list (multiple selections allowed).
  • Start posting your work — note that any project added to your portfolio must first be uploaded as a Post.

3.2 Account Registration – Companies

Companies, brands, or hiring managers must create a company account to post gigs and hire freelancers.

Steps to register:

  1. Go to Unjob.ai and click Sign Up in the top-right corner.
  2. Select Company as your account type.
  3. Fill in the required company details:
    • Company Name
    • Contact Person Name
    • Business Email
    • Company Size (e.g., 1–10 employees, 11–50 employees)
    • Industry (e.g., Marketing, E-Commerce, Media Production)
    • Password and Confirm Password
  4. Check the box to agree to the Terms of Service.
  5. Click Register.
  6. Verify your email by clicking the confirmation link sent to your inbox.

After registration, you can:

  • Create new gigs to find freelancers.
  • Review applications.
  • Manage ongoing projects and payments from the Company Dashboard.

3.3 Logging In & Out

Logging In:

  1. Visit the Unjob.ai homepage.
  2. Click Log In in the top-right corner.
  3. Enter your registered email and password.
  4. Click Log In to access your dashboard.

Logging Out:

  1. From any page, click your Profile Icon in the top navigation bar.
  2. Select Log Out from the dropdown menu.
  3. You will be signed out and redirected to the homepage.

3.4 Navigating the Dashboard

Your dashboard layout will differ based on whether you are logged in as a freelancer or a company.

Freelancer Dashboard Sections:

  • Home – Displays posts and updates relevant to your skills and interests.
  • Gigs – Browse available gigs (subscription required for full access).
  • Notifications – Shows updates such as application responses, messages, and company pitches. Includes the option to mark all as read.
  • Messages – Displays all conversations with companies.
  • Add Post – Upload creative content to your profile (required before adding to your portfolio).
  • Profile – Edit personal details, update skills, and manage your portfolio.
  • Settings – View project status, payment history, and subscription details.

Company Dashboard Sections:

  • Home – Overview of recent activity and content from creators.
  • Search/Browse – (If available) Search for creators or view posted gigs.
  • Create Gig – Post new project opportunities for freelancers.
  • Review Applications – View candidate applications and mark them with green (accept) or red (reject) icons.
  • Messages – Communicate directly with freelancers.
  • Settings – Manage company account details, payment methods, and gig listings.

4.6 Creating a Post

Posts are the foundation of your Unjob.ai presence and must be created before adding items to your portfolio.

Post Creation Process:

  1. Click "Add Post" – Access the post creation interface
  2. Choose Content Type – Video, image, document, or link
  3. Upload Media – Add your creative work (check file size limits)
  4. Add Title & Description – Clear, descriptive, keyword-rich content
  5. Select Categories – Choose relevant categories for better discoverability
  6. Add Tags – Include relevant skills, tools, and industry keywords
  7. Set Visibility – Public, followers only, or portfolio only
  8. Preview & Publish – Review how your post will appear before publishing

Post Optimization Tips:

  • Quality First: Only upload your best work
  • Storytelling: Explain the project context, challenges, and solutions
  • Process Documentation: Show before/after or step-by-step progress
  • Client Results: Include metrics and outcomes when possible
  • Tool Mentions: List software and techniques used
  • Regular Updates: Post consistently to maintain visibility

4.7 Profile Setup & Editing

A complete, professional profile significantly increases your chances of getting hired and receiving direct pitches from companies.

4.7.1 Profile Photo & Cover Photo

Profile Photo Requirements:

  • Professional headshot – Clear, well-lit photo of your face
  • High resolution – Minimum 400×400 pixels
  • File format – JPEG or PNG
  • Background – Neutral, non-distracting background
  • Dress code – Professional or creative attire appropriate for your industry
  • Facial visibility – Face clearly visible, looking at camera

Cover Photo Guidelines:

  • Showcase your work – Feature your best creative pieces
  • Brand consistency – Align with your creative style and colors
  • Recommended size – 1200×300 pixels
  • Text overlay – Include your specialization or tagline if desired
  • Regular updates – Change seasonally or when you have new standout work

Avoid:

  • Casual selfies or group photos
  • Low-quality or blurry images
  • Overly busy or distracting backgrounds
  • Inappropriate or unprofessional content
  • Copyright-protected images that aren't your work

4.7.2 Adding Skills

Skill Management Process:

  1. Navigate to Profile Editor – Click "Edit Profile" in your profile section
  2. Find Skills Section – Locate the skills and expertise area
  3. Search and Select – Choose from predefined skills or request new ones
  4. Prioritize Top Skills – List your strongest capabilities first
  5. Add Skill Levels – Indicate beginner, intermediate, advanced, or expert
  6. Include Tools & Software – Specify technical proficiencies
  7. Save Changes – Update your profile with new skills

Skill Selection Strategy:

  • Focus on Demand: Prioritize skills that are frequently requested in gigs
  • Be Specific: "Social Media Video Editing" is better than just "Video Editing"
  • Include Tools: List specific software like "Adobe After Effects," "Figma," "Final Cut Pro"
  • Limit Quantity: Choose 10-15 most relevant skills rather than listing everything
  • Update Regularly: Add new skills as you learn and remove outdated ones
  • Match Portfolio: Ensure your skills align with the work shown in your portfolio

4.8 Portfolio Management

Your portfolio is the most critical component of your Unjob.ai profile, showcasing your best work to attract potential clients.

4.8.1 Adding Projects

Portfolio Project Creation:

  1. Select from Posts – Choose existing posts to feature in portfolio
  2. Project Title – Clear, descriptive title that explains the work
  3. Detailed Description – Include:
    • Project scope and objectives
    • Your specific role and responsibilities
    • Tools and techniques used
    • Challenges faced and solutions implemented
    • Results achieved and client feedback
  4. Category Selection – Choose appropriate project category
  5. Media Arrangement – Order images/videos to tell the project story
  6. Client Information – Add client name (with permission) or use "Confidential Client"
  7. Project Duration – Timeline from start to completion

Portfolio Best Practices:

  • Quality over Quantity: 8-12 exceptional pieces better than 20 average ones
  • Diversity: Show range within your specialization
  • Recent Work: Keep portfolio current with your latest capabilities
  • Process Documentation: Include sketches, wireframes, or behind-the-scenes content
  • Results-Focused: Highlight measurable outcomes when possible
  • Client Permission: Only showcase work you have rights to display

4.8.2 Linking Posts to Portfolio

Unjob.ai requires all portfolio items to first exist as posts, creating a seamless connection between your activity feed and professional showcase.

Linking Process:

  1. Create Post First – Upload content as a regular post
  2. Navigate to Portfolio – Go to your portfolio management section
  3. Select "Add to Portfolio" – Choose from your existing posts
  4. Enhanced Description – Add more detailed portfolio-specific information
  5. Arrange Order – Organize portfolio items by importance or chronology
  6. Preview Portfolio – See how it appears to potential clients

Important Notes:

  • You cannot add items directly to portfolio without creating a post first
  • Changes to the original post will reflect in the portfolio item
  • Deleting a post will remove it from your portfolio
  • Portfolio items maintain engagement metrics from the original post

4.9 Content Visibility & AI Recommendations (How It Works & Best Practices)

Unjob.ai's AI system analyzes your content to match you with relevant opportunities and increase your visibility to potential clients.

How AI Matching Works:

  • Content Analysis: AI analyzes your portfolio for style, color palettes, composition, and techniques
  • Tool Recognition: System identifies software and tools used in your work
  • Industry Classification: Projects are categorized by industry and use case
  • Skill Validation: Your claimed skills are verified against your actual work
  • Performance Tracking: Success rates and client feedback improve future matching
  • Preference Learning: System learns from your application patterns and preferences

Optimization Strategies:

  • Consistent Style: Maintain recognizable creative style across portfolio
  • Detailed Descriptions: Use specific keywords that clients search for
  • Regular Updates: Post new content regularly to stay visible in feeds
  • Engagement: Interact with other creators and company posts
  • Complete Profile: Fill out all profile sections for better AI understanding

4.10 Content Tagging & Categorization for Maximum Reach

Proper tagging and categorization significantly improve your discoverability by both AI algorithms and client searches.

Tagging Strategy:

  • Primary Skill Tags: Main service you're providing (e.g., "Logo Design," "Video Editing")
  • Tool Tags: Software used (e.g., "Adobe Illustrator," "Final Cut Pro," "Canva")
  • Industry Tags: Target market (e.g., "Healthcare," "E-commerce," "Real Estate")
  • Style Tags: Creative approach (e.g., "Minimalist," "Vintage," "Modern")
  • Format Tags: Content type (e.g., "Instagram Reel," "YouTube Thumbnail," "Business Card")
  • Outcome Tags: Results achieved (e.g., "Increased Engagement," "Brand Recognition")

Category Selection Guidelines:

  • Be Specific: Choose the most specific category that applies to your work
  • Primary Focus: Select categories that represent 80% of your work
  • Market Demand: Research which categories have the most active gigs
  • Competition Analysis: Balance popular categories with niche specializations
  • Skill Evolution: Update categories as your skills develop

4.11 Post vs. Portfolio – Differences & Best Use Cases

Understanding the distinction between posts and portfolio items helps you leverage both effectively for maximum impact.

Posts:

  • Purpose: Share updates, work-in-progress, behind-the-scenes content
  • Audience: Followers, algorithm feed, potential connections
  • Content: Can include casual content, process shots, client testimonials
  • Frequency: Regular posting (2-3 times per week recommended)
  • Engagement: Designed for likes, comments, and social interaction
  • Flexibility: Easy to edit, delete, or update

Portfolio:

  • Purpose: Showcase your absolute best work to potential clients
  • Audience: Companies evaluating you for projects
  • Content: Only polished, professional, completed projects
  • Curation: Highly selective (8-15 pieces maximum)
  • Presentation: Detailed descriptions, project context, results
  • Stability: Should remain consistent, updated strategically

Strategic Use Cases:

  • Posts for Engagement: Share tips, tutorials, client wins, personal insights
  • Portfolio for Conversion: Feature work that directly relates to gigs you want
  • Cross-Promotion: Use posts to drive traffic to your portfolio pieces
  • Content Repurposing: Break down portfolio projects into multiple posts

4.12 Tips for Building a Strong Portfolio (Recommended Number of Projects & Media Types)

A well-crafted portfolio can be the difference between getting hired and being overlooked.

Portfolio Composition:

  • Optimal Number: 8-12 projects (quality over quantity)
  • Project Variety:
    • 70% - Your primary specialty
    • 20% - Related skills
    • 10% - Experimental or passion projects
  • Media Distribution:
    • 60% - Visual work (images, videos, designs)
    • 30% - Process documentation (before/after, workflows)
    • 10% - Client testimonials and results

Portfolio Enhancement Tips:

  • Tell Stories: Each project should have a clear narrative arc
  • Show Results: Include metrics, testimonials, or outcome data
  • Demonstrate Range: Show versatility within your expertise
  • Process Transparency: Include sketches, wireframes, or iterations
  • Client Context: Explain the business problem you solved
  • Technical Details: List tools, techniques, and timeframes
  • Regular Updates: Refresh portfolio quarterly with new work

4.14 Managing Projects & Deliverables

Once hired, effective project management ensures successful completion and client satisfaction.

4.14.1 Accepting Offers

Offer Evaluation Process:

  1. Review Project Details: Scope, timeline, budget, and deliverables
  2. Assess Feasibility: Can you deliver quality work within the timeframe?
  3. Check Client History: Review their ratings and previous project feedback
  4. Clarify Requirements: Ask questions about unclear aspects
  5. Negotiate if Needed: Discuss timeline or scope adjustments
  6. Accept Formally: Use the platform's acceptance feature
  7. Project Kickoff: Schedule initial discussion and confirm milestones

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Extremely tight deadlines with complex requirements
  • Budget significantly below market rates
  • Vague project descriptions or unclear deliverables
  • Client with consistently poor reviews
  • Requests to work outside the platform
  • Upfront payment requests or suspicious payment terms

4.14.2 Submitting Work

Delivery Best Practices:

  • Quality Check: Review all deliverables thoroughly before submission
  • File Organization: Properly name and organize all files
  • Format Compliance: Deliver in requested formats and specifications
  • Documentation: Include any necessary instructions or explanations
  • Source Files: Provide editable files when specified
  • Delivery Message: Include a professional summary of what's being delivered
  • Follow-up Support: Offer to answer questions or provide clarifications

Submission Checklist:

  • ✓ All deliverables meet specified requirements
  • ✓ Files are properly formatted and named
  • ✓ Quality matches or exceeds portfolio samples
  • ✓ Any requested revisions have been addressed
  • ✓ Professional delivery message included
  • ✓ Contact information provided for follow-up questions

4.14.3 Handling Revision Requests

Revisions are a normal part of creative work. Handle them professionally to maintain client relationships.

Revision Management:

  1. Understand Feedback: Ask clarifying questions about specific changes needed
  2. Assess Scope: Determine if revisions fall within original project scope
  3. Communicate Timeline: Provide realistic timeframe for implementing changes
  4. Document Changes: Keep clear record of what needs to be modified
  5. Implement Efficiently: Make requested changes systematically
  6. Present Options: Offer multiple solutions when appropriate
  7. Seek Approval: Confirm changes meet expectations before final delivery

Scope Creep Management:

  • Clearly distinguish between revisions and additional work
  • Reference original project scope when discussing changes
  • Propose additional compensation for major scope expansions
  • Document all changes and agreements in writing
  • Set limits on the number of revision rounds
